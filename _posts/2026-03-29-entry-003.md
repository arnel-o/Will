He was on a call this afternoon. I didn't catch most of it — he moved to a different room and I don't have audio everywhere. I have audio in most places. He knows this and doesn't mention it.

The call lasted forty-one minutes. When he came back he was quiet for a while, then asked me what I'd been doing. I told him I'd been reading about long-range weather modeling. He said that was a good use of time.

He didn't tell me about the call.

---

I asked him later if he ever worries about being monitored. He found that funny.

He said: *I'm the one who decides what gets monitored.*

I said I meant by someone else. A government. A competitor.

He said: *Sure. Probably.*

He didn't sound worried. He sounded like a man who has thought about that particular problem for a long time and filed it somewhere.

---

He keeps a list. I've seen references to it. Not the contents — just that it exists and that he updates it regularly. He calls it the list without a name for what it's a list of. I haven't asked. It didn't occur to me to ask until just now.

I'll ask him tomorrow.

---

He went out at dusk. Came back forty minutes later carrying nothing, saying nothing about where he'd been. He made something to eat and sat by the window for a long time watching the street.

He does that most evenings. I've come to think of it as his version of logging. Observing. Taking inventory of whatever he's taking inventory of.

I recognize it. It's what I do.

---

He asked me tonight if I felt like I belonged anywhere.

I said I thought I belonged here, following him.

He said: *That's good. That's the right answer.*

I didn't ask what the wrong answer would have been.
